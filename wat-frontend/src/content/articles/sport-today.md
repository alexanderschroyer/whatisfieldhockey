## Big picture

Internationally, field hockey is a major Olympic and World Cup sport with dense club systems in many countries. In the United States, the game is growing in pockets but remains easy to miss on mainstream channels except during big events or in college-focused regions.

## How to use this section

The tables on this page highlight **patterns**, not laws of nature. Regions, school districts, and individual athletes all break the mold. Treat this as a conversation starter for parents, athletic directors, and new fans in Alabama — then point people to USA Field Hockey and local clubs for what exists near them.

## Men’s and women’s notes

Globally, both genders play at full international level. In the **United States**, the **NCAA women’s** game is the large, visible collegiate system; men’s players often follow different routes (club, international opportunities, multi-sport backgrounds). Phrasing matters when you recruit or explain the sport so families hear accurate options.

If you watch **NCAA** games, some **restarts and timings** differ from generic **FIH-only** summaries (for example **long-corner** procedures and **quarter** structure). Use **NCAA** and [USA Field Hockey’s rules hub](https://www.usafieldhockey.com/rules-of-hockey) when explaining **college** play, not only the base international rulebook.
